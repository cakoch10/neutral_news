While pubs are closing across the country at an alarming rate there's been a dramatic growth of micropubs in the last decade.

These small alternatives to pubs hark back to the old ale houses - they are often just one room and dedicated to cask ale.

A new guide published this month catalogues how they are often to be found in unusual places.

The Today programme visited one in a tiny former cab office on the platform of a train station in Surrey.

It fits just two customers.
